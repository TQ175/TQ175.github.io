What is Volleyball?

Volleyball is a game played between two teams of six, each team trying to make a ball fall into each other's side while ensuring the ball goes over the net that divides the court Each team is only allowed three touches on the ball before having to return the ball to the other side of the court. There should be no consecutive touches of the ball by the same player.

Roles

  • Setter

    • The setter is essentially the "Maestro" of the game as they make the decisions on where to attack and who gets to spike. The setter usually gets the second touch and has to make use of all their attackers.They control most of the game and has the most responsibilty to ensure their team is in peak condition.

  • Outside Hitter

    • The outside hitter spikes from the left side of the court and has to have a high endurance to jumping as they need to noy only jump to spike, but also block attacks from the other team if need be. They are important for the offence and ensures that they are versatile enough to hit any type of ball set to them.

  • Opposite Hitter

    • The opposite hitter spikes from the right side of the court and has to be powerful enough to attack not only from the right side, but also from the back row. Opposite hitters also join in blocking if need be but usually are focusing on attacking the ball.

  • Middle Blocker

    • The middle blockers sepcialise in blocking the opponent's attack, either by completely stopping the ball in hopes the ball falls into the opponent's court or to slow the ball down for the other players to receive a easier ball. When blocking, even if the ball touches one's arms or hands, it does not count as a "touch" so middle blockers can make use of this to help their teams set up a better a better attack.

  • Libero

    • The Libero is the "Guardian" of the team, having to focus solely on receiving the ball, no matter what. Unlike other players, the libero stays on the floor for the most of it and can only play in the back row. This means that they have to substitute with another player every time a rotation calls for it. The libero usually wears a jersey of a different color than their teammates and have to be agile and fast as they have to cover a large area of the court while "digging" up spikes.

Rotation

Players have to rotate in a clockwise direction by one "position" after winning a point from the other team. If a team scores consecutive points, they do not need to rotate again. The main thing is the Overlap Rule. The rule states that before an opponent's serve, players are not allowed to 'overlap' with the player directly in front, behind, or beside them.

Brazil

An overpowering team that has dominated the sport for almost two decades. Volleyball is a major sport in Brazil, so it is no surprise that this team of giants can be found easily. With coaches like Bernardo guiding its players, the Brazillian team have been able to secure its dominance throughout the years, using their adaptable playstyle to focus on the weaknesses of their opponents, and their all-roundedness in tersm of technique to outplay their opponents.

Japan

The "underdog" team that never fails to keep the audiences entertained and coming back for more. The Japanese team are widely known for their explosive, fast and scrappy playstyle, keeping their opponents on their toes. The players on the Japanese team are regarded as short by normal volleyball standards, and yet, they seem to still have the freedom of choice during games, be it for blocking, setting or spiking. They are able to think fast and be quick on the offence. Thier unpredictable yet explosive playstyle has brought them far in the world of volleyball.

United States of America

A strong and powerful tower of a team that has shown its strength time and time again, it is no surprise that the American team are one of the easiest teams to root for. Thier emphasis on a strong foundation has been the key to their strentgh, as they light a path for young athletes to hone their skills and guide them closely. With such a supportive system to create such skilled athletes, those same athletes have a chance to grow even more by playing against each other, allowing for the final team to be forever incomplete as they keep on growing.

Turkey

An overall explosive and powerful team, the Turkish team has steadily and consistently grown over the years. They have shown immense progress through much hard work and dedication, forming a united front as the team pushes each other to the limit and helping each other, be it on or off the court. Thier sheer dominance at the net, housing canons for players, it is no wonder they were gifted with the name "Sultans of the net". This name not only spurred the team on to acheive more but also drove more people in Turkey to pick up the sport.

Italy

A team driven by each and every play, playing as one body, the Italian team has yet to let the crowd down with thier spectacular teamwork. Even with a star-player in their midst, they ensure each player imposes a threat to thier opponents, reminding them not to let thier guard down. This was achieved through not only rigorous training, but also through mental shaping. The Italian team have all came to a consensus of the "hear and now" mentality, which drives them to focus solely on the moment, raising their senses in the game and allowing them to break free of any anxiety and hesitation they may have.

United States of America

Persistent and strong, the American team dominates the court as they continually hone their skills. As the country has already lit a bright path for any aspiring athlete to excel, along with a supportive community, young athletes get a chance to not only build a strong foundation, but also improve at a rapid speed. With such a strong foundation, it is no wonder why the American team have taken the court with ease, switching to the offence quick and ensuring that their defence stays up.
